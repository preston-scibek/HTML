The Great Ray Lewis

Ray Lewis is perhaps the greatest Raven to have ever lived. He stayed with the time his entire career(17 years), was drafted in the team's first draft, and was the only member of the team to be on both Super Bowl winning teams. Ray Lewis inspired his teammates with his hard work, dedication, and charisma. He is also involved in charity work

Statistics

  • Ray Lewis was the second linebacker to win Super Bowl MVP and was the first to win it on the winning team.
  • Ray Lewis was selected to 13 Pro Bowls and was named AP All Pro 10 times.
  • Ray Lewis was the NFL Defensive Player of the Year in 2000 and 2003. He is the 6th player to win the award multiple times.
  • Ray Lewis was a three time AFC Defensive Player of the Year winning it in 2000, 2001, 2003.
  • Ray Lewis was Alumni Linebacker of the year in 1999, and 2003 as voted on by retired NFL linebackers.
  • Ray Lewis was part of the NFL 2000's All-Decade Team, which is based on ones performance from 2000-2009.
  • Ray Lewis is the Baltimore Ravens all time career leader in tackles
  • Ray Lewis's 13 Pro Bowls is an NFL record for Inside/Middle Linebackers and his 10 AP All-Pro selections are tied with Lawrence Taylor for the most of a Linebacker.
  • Ray Lewis has the NFL record with 51 tackles in a single post season.

Charity Work

Ray Lewis found the Ray Lewis 52 foundation a non profit corporation whose goal is to provide personal and economic assistance to disadvantaged youth. The foundation has adopted 10 families for holidays, has had celebrity auctions and bowling tournaments, as well as participated in Thanksgiving food drives and Ray's Summer Days. Ray has been involved in pressing political, business, and philanthropic leaders for a stronger commitment to disability sports both in the US and the developing world.
